Purpose

This Policy aims to ensure that XPS networks are designed, implemented and managed according to best practice security standards in order to protect XPS data and IT services, whilst allowing XPS to utilise innovative and evolving technologies.

Scope

This Policy is primarily directed at XPS IT Support staff and Administrators who are responsible for the development and maintenance of the company’s IT infrastructure. It also extends to all employees, external contractors and other 3rd parties who undertake activities on the IT infrastructure.

It is the responsibility of every individual to whom this policy applies to adhere to it and fully comply with its requirements.

Preamble

All network environments change over time, whether the change is planned or unplanned. Change Control Policies help to minimise the inadvertent introduction of security vulnerabilities when implementing planned, unplanned, or recovery changes to a network environment.

Change control is essential in all environments. A change in a staging or development environment that creates a design flaw will typically be replicated to the production environment. Lack of change control policies for all environments can cause flawed configuration changes or code enhancements in the production environment.

Another danger to production environments is disaster recovery situations. If changes made to a production environment are not properly documented, as per a change control policy, systems that have been recovered might be put into production without having been properly hardened. Without proper change control procedures in place, systems could be placed back into production after planned maintenance has been performed, without having been properly secured.

This policy specifically addresses changes made to Xn protel Systems (“XPS”) hosted application environments.

Controls

Network management

  1. Only System Administrators and Computer Support staff of XPS IT Department are permitted to connect / disconnect systems, computers, switches, and / or any piece of IT related equipment to and from the company’s IT network or infrastructure.
  2. All changes to the corporate IT network or infrastructure must be fully documented, in line with the XPS Change Control Process.
  3. Network diagrams must be maintained and up to date, and include all connections into the cardholder data environment as well as all permitted wireless networks.
  4. The relevant Change Request Form must be completed and filed BEFORE any change is made (XPS Change Control Process). This Form should include, where relevant:
    1. A “Risk Assessment” detailing the risks of making /not making the change.
    2. A “Roll Back Plan”.
  5. All switches, hubs, routers, patch panels, firewalls, servers and mass storage devices must be physically situated in secure locations with access restricted to the IT Infrastructure Team (e.g. the “Comms Room”). These locations must be kept locked at ALL times.

Network security

  1. All users will be issued with a user name and password to form a Unique User Identifier (UUID).
  2. All vendor-supplied defaults must be changed before any system is installed on the network including, but not limited to, default passwords, default SNMP strings and unnecessary accounts.
  3. Wireless networks must be logically separated from the hosted application environment i.e. by Firewall(s).
  4. Within the networks outside of the HOSTED APPLICATION ENVIRONMENT, the IT Infrastructure Department must authorize the placement of any wireless access points and dial-in modems. The use of these devices must be logged.
  5. Connection from any wireless network to XPS network, even from remote workers, is to be discouraged as a security risk (potential man in the middle attack).
  6. Authorised and unauthorised wireless access points are to be detected and identified on a quarterly basis. Any detection of the unauthorised wireless access points is to be reported to the IT Infrastructure Director immediately, who will then trigger the wireless incident response plan.
  7. Remote workers are forbidden to access, process or transmit customer’s credit / debit card details.
  8. All remote access to the Company’s infrastructure requires two-factor authentication.
  9. Only machines that have been registered as Mail Servers by XPS IT Infrastructure Department will be permitted to send and / or receive email directly whilst on site.
  10. In exceptional circumstances, for example internal vulnerability testing, it may be necessary for a specialist third party individual to connect their own equipment to the hosted application environment. In these circumstances, permission must be obtained from the IT Infrastructure Team and the change request process followed (see XPS Change Control Policy). A member of XPS IT Infrastructure Department must be present to oversee the operation at all times. (Note that it is forbidden for XPS employees to connect their own equipment to the IT Network under any circumstances.)

Transmission over untrusted networks

Confidential and sensitive information must be encrypted during transmission over networks in which is it easy and common for the data to be intercepted, modified or diverted. Examples of strong encryption that is acceptable include:

  • Transport Layer Security (TLS 1.1 and above)
  • Internet Protocol Security (IPSEC)

Only trusted keys and or certificates are prohibited during transmission of sensitive information over un- trusted networks, and the protocol in use is to only support secure configurations. The use of insecure protocol versions, such as SSL, SSH v1.0 and TLS v1.0 are not permitted.

Email transmission of confidential information

Confidential and sensitive information is never to be sent unencrypted through email. Employees, with a valid business justification, must be issued email encryption software by the Information Security Department.

Anti-Virus

  1. Anti-virus software must be deployed on all systems commonly affected by malicious software and all anti-virus programs deployed on the network must be capable of detecting, removing, and protecting against all known types of malicious software (for example, viruses, Trojans, worms, spyware, adware and rootkits).
  2. Anti-virus software must be updated automatically.
  3. Non-system-administrator users must not be able to disable or otherwise amend the configuration of anti-virus software.
  4. All machines connecting to the corporate network must be scanned for viruses and malware before connection.
  5. All Anti-Virus software logs must be retained for a minimum of 12 months. The most recent 3 months (as a minimum) must be available for immediate inspection.

System “patching” policy

  1. All system components and software must be protected from known vulnerabilities by having the latest vendor-supplied security patches installed.
  2. An analysis of vulnerabilities must be performed. Systems and software must be evaluated to verify currency of the patch and update levels. Online resources should be consulted as part of this process, for example:
    • The US Computer Emergency Response Team (www.us-cert.gov/ncas)
    • The National Vulnerability Database (http://nvd.nist.gov)
  3. Security patches must be installed within 30 days of release.
  4. Windows machines are updated using Windows Software Update Services (WSUS). See Appendix A for details of the WSUS patching process.
  5. Linux must be checked weekly for new security vulnerabilities. All recommended updates must be applied. See Appendix B for details of the process.
  6. After any patches have been applied a vulnerability scan must be performed.

System “logging” policy

  1. To ensure the integrity of log files within XPS, clocks on all systems must be synchronised with a central time server(s) inside the organisations network.
  2. Only the designated central time server(s) shall receive time signals from external sources, and time signals from external sources are to be received only from industry-accepted, time sources and are required to be based on International Atomic Time or UTC.
  3. Where there is more than one designated time server, the time servers are required to peer with one another to maintain an accurate time.
  4. Employees who are members of the IT Infrastructure team are the only department with access to Time data. The justification for this access, is that they are required to troubleshoot and maintain the clock synchronisation systems, that synchronise time to all other devices throughout the XPS network.
  5. Changes to time settings on critical systems must be logged and monitored. Any changes to time settings should be reviewed immediately.
  6. The following log files, on devices within the hosted application environment, must be reviewed at least daily. The review of these log files can be conducted either manually or by using log tools.
    1. All security events.
    2. Logs of all system components that store, process, or transmit CHD and/or SAD.
    3. Logs of all critical system components.
    4. Logs of all servers and system components that perform security functions.
    5. Log files for other system components must be reviewed at least monthly.
  7. Any exceptions or anomalies are to be reported to the IT Infrastructure Director, who will follow up and take action if required. Such action may involve adding items to the risk register or invoking the incident response plan.
  8. All audit logs shall be retained for at least 1 year, with a minimum of 3 months immediately available.

 

Exceptions

As per the Information Security Policy, any policy exceptions must be formally documented and presented for review and sign-off by the Information Security Manager. All such exceptions are valid for a period of no longer than one year, upon which they will be subject to further review and formal acceptance.

Enforcement

Any individual found to have violated this policy may be subject to disciplinary action, up to and including termination of contract
