Policy Statement
Purpose

This policy sets out the rules under which access to XPS hosted applications are provided, controlled and managed. These requirements are designed to minimise the potential exposure of company and client information resources from damages that may result from unauthorised use.

Damages include the wilful or unintended modification and loss of confidential or restricted data, intellectual property, computational damage and financial liabilities incurred as a result of those losses.

Scope

This product policy applies to all XPS employees, customers, and third-parties with access to XPS hosted business solutions. This policy also applies to XPS personnel responsible for the ongoing maintenance and support of its hosted business solutions.

1. Controls

1.1 Access control

1.1.1 Access to XPS hosted systems must be controlled by a centralised authentication, authorisation and accounting system, (i.e. Windows Active Directory domains).

1.1.2 Local authentication is not permitted unless a valid business or technical justification has been assessed and approved in accordance with the established risk management process.

1.1.3 Shared accounts are not permitted unless a valid business or technical justification has been assessed and approved in accordance with the established risk management process.

1.1.3 Where a shared account can be justified, a process will be deployed and documented to include:

  1. Purpose of account usage;
  2. the name(s) and contact details of all individuals that will use the account;
  3. expected duration of account usage; and
  4. Account passwords are reset when any one person no longer has a valid reason for usage.

1.2 Access provisioning

1.2.1 All accounts must be associated with a single owner and where possible assign to a group.

1.2.2 Access requests must be approved by an appropriate authority prior to implementation by the IT systems and application administrators.

1.2.3 Where possible, role-based access controls must be implemented for user authentication. Users inherit a pre-defined set of access rights.

1.2.4 Account and relative access rights must be removed or disabled when a user no longer requires access due to changing roles or leaving employment.

1.2.5 Accounts for XPS contractors must always have an expiration date aligned with the contract period.

1.2.6 Emergency provisions should exist whereupon access requests can be approved and documented retrospectively.

1.2.7 The authenticating systems (i.e. Active Directory) must reflect the current list of approved individuals.

1.3 Requests for remote access

1.3.1 All remote access requests used to connect to XPS hosted services must be approved by an appropriate authority (e.g., [TBC]). Remote access is controlled and implemented according to the established Network Security Policy and subject to the following restrictions:

  1. Users are securely authenticated (via a username/password pair prior to the establishment of a remote connection.
  2. “Highly Sensitive”, “Sensitive”, and “Private” data must be encrypted when the data is transferred through public networks (i.e., Internet).
  3. Remote access accounts with privileged rights for administrative use must be limited to authorised personnel and strictly controlled via:
    1. Regular monitoring of event logs.
    2. Periodic reviews, at least annually, of access rights to verify the ongoing appropriateness of access rights.
    3. Users must not attempt to circumvent remote access (VPN) security controls. Any deviations must be addressed from a risk perspective and approved by the remote access and end point system business owner.

1.4 User responsibilities

1.4.1 All users are personally responsible for the use of their account and must:

  • Select strong passwords.
  • Change the password if it is known or suspected that an account has been compromised.
  • Keep passwords secure and never reveal them under any circumstances.
  • Not attempt to use any account other than their own, unless for legitimate support or security incident purposes.
  • Not share their user account with other individuals.
  • Users are cautioned against the use of passwords that can be easily guessed or obtained using collateral information (e.g. names, birth dates, telephone numbers, family information, etc).

1.5 Account Management

1.5.1 A standard Naming Convention scheme is followed when creating accounts in order to facilitate the accountability of user actions.

1.5.2 User account management controls must be established to lock user accounts after a defined number of failed authentication attempts:

 

Accont Management Contol Item Account Management Policy
Lockout threshold 10 consecutive invalid attempts
Lockout duration 30-minutes
Inactivity lockout (based on account type/role)
Maximum user screen lockout inactivity 30-minutes (before password re-try)

 

1.6 Password management

1.6.1 Passwords are mandatory for all user accounts on all networked and standalone information systems including operating systems, applications, databases, end user computing hardware and mobile devices (notebooks, tablets etc):

 

Password Control Item Password Policy
Minimum password length Minimum 8-characters (recommended 14-characters or more).
Password complexity

Password must comply with any 3 of the following:

  • Minimum 1 alpha (upper)
  • Minimum 1 alpha (lower)
  • Minimum 1 numeric character
  • Minimum 1 special character
  • NOT contain account name, first name, surname or full name.
Max. password age (to limit password utility) 180-days
Min. password age (to prevent reverting to old password) 5-days
Password history (to prevent password reuse) Last 12 passwords.

1.6.2 Users must change their password at first login

1.6.3 Passwords must not be displayed in clear text on any input or fixed screens, hard copy reports or electronic files.

1.6.4 Any default passwords shipped with any application must be changed immediatly upon installation.

2. Control exceptions

All exemption requests must be reviewed, assessed and approved by the relevant business stakeholders
