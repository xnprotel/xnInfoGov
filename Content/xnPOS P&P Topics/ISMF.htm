Policy Statement
Purpose

To ensure XPS cloud hosted business solutions can be used with confidence and in the knowledge that information is complete, accurate and protected from misuse, damage, loss or unauthorised disclosure.

This policy and its underpinning policy statements support the ongoing monitoring, identification and mitigation of security vulnerabilities, threats and risks in order to protect XPS information technology and communications assets.
Scope

This policy applies to XPS staff, its customers, and any third-party with access to XPS cloud hosted solutions. These policies apply to all hosted applications containing information that might impact customers in the event data was lost.

 

1. Preamble

This policy is concerned with the management and security of XPS information assets and the use made of these assets by its staff and others who may legitimately process XPS information on behalf of XPS.

 

 

 

Xn protel Systems ("XPS") helps world-class hospitality companies in over 50 countries to optimise revenue generation, simplify service operations and enhance the quality of guest communications.

XPS is committed to achieving and maintaining the trust of our customers. Central to this mission is the provision of a robust security and privacy program that carefully considers data protection matters across all of our services, including data submitted by customers to our services (“Customer Data”).

Cloud-hosted business solutions and the information and communication technologies they are built upon are of critical importance to core business activities and must be protected.

This Policy informs all users of XPS ICT resources of their rights and responsibilities and establishes our commit to preserving the confidentiality, integrity, and availability of all cloud hosted services.

2. Services Covered

This documentation describes the architecture of, the security and privacy-related audits and certifications received for, and the administrative, technical and physical controls applicable to the service branded as xnPOS (the "Service.")

3. Policy statements

2.1 Data backup

Data Backups are primarily a preventative measure to protect against loss of data resulting from system failure (disaster or other), virus or malware attack and system or human error.

Backups are an essential control and safeguard to ensure availability of XPS data being stored, processed or transmitted via information technology communication systems.

Statement: Data must be backed up on a regular basis, protected from unauthorised access or modification during storage, and available to be recovered in a timely manner in the event of incident or disaster. See "DSMF_01 Data Backup" on page 1.

2.2 User access

Preventative measures designed to ensure only authorised users are granted access to XPS hosted systems. Unauthorised access may result in a security breaches resulting in the release, destruction or modification of sensitive data

Statement: All access-related requests must be logged, reviewed and approved in accordance with the defined user access management process. This includes requests for new user accounts, requests concerning account priviledges and requests concerning the removal of access rights. See "DSMF_02 User access" on page 1.

x.x Subpoena Policy

XPS accepts subpoenas sent via fax, email, regular or overnight mail and hand-delivery. XPS will respond to all valid subpoenas or court orders from entities and courts who have proper subpoena power and jurisdiction over XPS.

The following are the basic requirements:

  • Times and dates
  • IP information
  • Domain/website/host information
  • Whether the subpoena may be disclosed to the customer (unless it specifies that the request must remain confidential, XPS will notify its customer of the pending subpoena)

XPS understands the time-sensitive nature of each subpoena request. Unless we are required to notify our customer of the subpoena and allow them the opportunity to object or otherwise respond, we have a turn-around time of 24-hours or less, excluding special circumstances and/or in a situation where there is missing or incomplete information in the request.

XPS will provide responses to the subpoena via fax, email, or regular mail; therefore, a preferred method of delivery and accurate contact information must be provided by the issuing party. XPS will disable accounts engaged in illegal activity, even if that activity is brought to our attention through a request for records. If disabling or restricting user access to the user's profile will jeopardise the investigation, the issuing party should clearly specify "DO NOT DISABLE UNTIL DD/MM/YYYY" their request.
