Policy Statement
Purpose

This Policy describes permissible and impermissible use of the xnPOS business solution and to ensure that users act in compliance with applicable laws, rules and regulations.

Scope

This Policy applies to Customer use of and access to any XPS services or products (“Services”) made available through XPS' hosted Data Centres. The Customer agrees to comply with this Acceptable Use Policy by using Services, which may be amended, modified or updated from time to time.

1. Controls

1.1 User accounts

Authorised users

Only an individual who has been assigned a unique user name and password ("Authorised user") is entitled to access XPS cloud hosted services. The customer is responsible and liable for any violation of the terms and conditions of this Policy.

Login credentials

The customer is solely responsible for the security of application login credentials and all activity resulting from their use. The customer acknowledges and agrees the XPS may provide access to its cloud hosted services to anyone using customer login credentials or who is otherwise authorised by the customer to use or access those services on the customer's behalf. The customer will immediately disable an Authorised user's access when the Authorised user is no longer authorised to use the login credentials or otherwise use or access XPS hosted services.

Policy violations

Where a violation of the terms and conditions of this Policy is either suspected or alleged, the customer will notify XPS immediately and shall reasonably assist in any investigative action. XPS reserves the right to suspend, modify, restrict or terminate any or all services or customer access if XPS suspects or determines that any Authorised user has violated the terms and conditions of this Policy.

Prohibited activities

General prohibitions

The customer must not (and will not permit) any employee or third-party service provider who has been authorised to access XPS cloud services to:

  1. copy, modify, adapt, alter, translate, display or use the services, except as expressly allowed herein;
  2. sub-license, lease, rent, loan, distribute, resell, provide access to XPS cloud services on a time-share or service bureau basis, or otherwise transfer the XPS cloud services, including, as applicable, the documentation, software, associated user interfaces, help resources, and any related technology or services XPS makes available via the xnPOS portal site, and all updates and upgrades thereto, if any, to any third party;
  3. reverse engineer, decompile, disassemble, or otherwise derive or determine or attempt to derive or determine the source code (or the underlying ideas, algorithms, structure or organisation) of portal services;
  4. contest or do or aid others in contesting or doing anything which impairs the validity of any proprietary or intellectual property rights, title, or interest of XPS in and to the Services;
  5. obliterate, alter, or remove any proprietary or intellectual property notices from the Services in physical or electronic form;
  6. use the Services to interfere with, gain unauthorised access to, or otherwise violate the security of XPS or another party’s server, network, network access, personal computer or control devices, software or data, or other system, or to attempt to do any of the foregoing;
  7. use the Services to violate any applicable law, statute, ordinance or regulation; or
  8. use the Services to transmit, publish, or distribute any material or information that
    1. contains a computer virus or other code, files, or programs designed to disrupt or interfere with the functioning of the Services,
    2. for which Customer does not have all necessary rights and licenses, including any material or information that infringes, violates, or misappropriates the intellectual property rights of any third party, or
    3. that is or that may reasonably be perceived as being harmful, threatening, offensive, obscene, or otherwise objectionable.

Customer information

Privacy and security regulations

Uploading, inputting or storing information protected under applicable privacy or security regulations is prohibited. Customer shall not upload, input or store non-public sensitive or personally identifiable information including but not limited to driver’s license numbers, passport numbers, social security, personal tax ID or similar numbers, personal credit card, and personal financial or health information into the services.

Service monitoring

Resource usage and account access

XPS will take all reasonable precautions to protect customer security and privacy. Consistent with these purposes, XPS will only access a Customer's end-user accounts in the following circumstances:

  • When an employee is unexpectedly absent from work and access is required for legitimate business purposes or work health and safety reasons.
  • When XPS reasonably suspectes that an indivudual is not compliying with this or other policies or procedures.
  • For use in legal procedings or as required by law (e.g. compliance with a Notice to Produce or a subpoena).
  • For IT security purposes.

Consistnant with this approach, access to an individual's account will only be granted with the approval of the Group Technical Infrastructure Director and the affected customer.

Statistical analysis

XPS may monitor, collect and use data pertaining to the use of the cloud hosted services to extract, compile, synthesise, and analyze any non-personally and non-Customer identifiable data or information resulting from Customer’s use of the Services (“Statistical Data”).

XPS may only use Statistical Data for research, development (including to improve the Services and develop new products) and marketing purposes and may only publicly disclose such Statistical Data in an aggregated format that in no way identifies Customer, any particular Authorized User, and/or Customer's confidential information (e.g., Kyriba may disclose statistical and performance information related to the provision and operation of the Services, including aggregate number and volume of transactions processed for all its hosted customers).

Employee exit

XPS general procedure

When an employee leaves XPS, supervisors must ensue that all access to XPS administrative systems and networks is removed or amended as required upon the employee's departure.

It may be necessary for a supervisor to access work files or email accounts after an employee’s departure in order to preserve continuity of work. Depending on the cicumstances, an employee might be given the opportunity to remove any personal files or email from company laptops, devices or cloud storage prior to their departure .

Customer general procedures

When an authorised account holder leaves, the customer must notifiy XPS immediately.

Control exceptions

All exemption requests must be reviewed, assessed and approved by the relevant business stakeholders
